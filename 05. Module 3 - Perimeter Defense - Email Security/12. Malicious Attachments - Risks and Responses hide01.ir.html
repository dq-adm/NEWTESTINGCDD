Perimeter Defense - Email Security > [T2]: Malicious Attachments: Risks and Responses

 

Attackers attach malicious files to emails and send them to their targets. When the recipient opens the file, it executes a series of commands to take over the recipient's computer and propagates to other systems in the network. Malicious attachments do not have a specific type; they can come in any format, such as Microsoft Office documents, PDF files, and executables.
 

Exploits 

One way to utilize attachments for malicious purposes is to exploit vulnerable applications. For example, attackers can utilize new vulnerabilities discovered in Microsoft Word by preparing a Word document that exploits the discovered vulnerability and sending it to the victim.

 

Once the recipient opens the attachment, it gets processed by the vulnerable version of Microsoft Word installed on the victim's machine, and the attacker gains access to the computer. This attack is not limited to Microsoft office only. Attackers can target any client-side application on the victims machine, such as Adobe, FileZilla FTP client, or Putty SSH client.
