What is OSSEC?

 

OSSEC (Open Source Security) is the most well-known and used open-source HIDS (Host-based Intrusion Detection System). It can run on most operating systems, including and not limited to Windows, OSX, BSD, and Linux. 

OSSEC can be used as FIM (File Integrity Monitoring), check for RootKits, and active response to take immediate actions based on triggered alerts. 

Logs from OSSEC are easy to understand and can get shipped to logging solutions like ELK based on the tuning you have performed on the policy level, which will be covered later on this topic.
 

What will we learn in this lab?

  1. Overview of OSSEC architecture and configs
  2. Detect webshells dropped in the apache root folder (FIM)
  3. Detect persistence and privilege escalation (HIDS)
