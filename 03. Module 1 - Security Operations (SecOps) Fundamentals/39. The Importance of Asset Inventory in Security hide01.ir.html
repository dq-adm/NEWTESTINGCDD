Assets Inventory Platforms

You can't secure what you can't see! This well-known quote shows how critical it is to have an accurate and comprehensive list of all organization's assets so that you can protect them. Visibility is a very crucial element of any security program, if not the most, that enables defenders to do their job correctly because you cannot protect what you do not know about! Anything connected to the network is an asset, be it a laptop, server, workstation, mobile device, router, switch, firewall, wireless access point, etc. Assets Inventory can include both hardware and software assets. 

A concerning challenge for Security Operation Centers is Shadow IT. Shadow IT refers to IT devices and tools deployed without the security team's knowledge and approval, resulting in visibility gaps. For example, an IT administrator spins up a temporary server to test and evaluate a new solution the IT team is considering buying. Depending on the solution, this server may require public internet access. Imagine what could happen if a server that does not comply with the security policy has been published on the internet (i.e., missing security controls such as AntiVirus, Firewall, secure configurations, etc.)? In the real world, attackers use these forgotten and unmonitored devices to compromise the network and pivot to more critical assets. 

Whatever we say, we will not be able to emphasize enough the criticality and importance of assets inventory. Do you know that assets inventory is the 1st item on the list of 20 critical security controls provided by the center for internet security? 

According to the Improving the Effectiveness of the Security Operations Center study: 

"The top barrier to SOC success, according to 65 percent of respondents, is the lack of visibility into the IT security infrastructure, and the top reason for SOC ineffectiveness, according to 69 percent, is the lack of visibility into network traffic." 

Another study conducted by Spin Technology suggests that: 

  • ~49% of cyberattacks were due to shadow IT.
  • The number of applications used in the company is 14.6 times bigger than the number known to the IT department.
  • ~67% of individuals and teams brought their tools to their organization, and ~40% used unauthorized software daily.


Almost all organizations know the importance of having an accurate and updated asset inventory. However, the majority fail partially or entirely in maintaining an asset inventory because of many challenges. For example, many asset inventory platforms, such as LanSweeper, work by scanning the whole network to detect all connected devices. Imagine how much time and bandwidth it takes to scan big networks! Sometimes, these scans may break some network devices or systems and make them irresponsive. 

Creating and maintaining an asset inventory is usually part of a bigger asset management policy, the process to follow when connecting an asset to the network. The success of obtaining and maintaining an asset inventory is not limited to the tool used. Still, it is influenced by other factors, such as the quality and enforcement of the asset management policy. It is a mega project; deploying asset inventory platforms alone is insufficient.
