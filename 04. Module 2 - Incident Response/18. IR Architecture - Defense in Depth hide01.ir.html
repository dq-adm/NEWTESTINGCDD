Defense in Depth

 

Figure 1

Defense-in-depth (DID) is a cybersecurity concept where a series of defensive mechanisms are layered one after the other to provide redundant protection. DID assumes a defense layer may fail at any time, and additional layers should complement failed layers.

DID improves overall security, but how can it enhance IR specifically? Each security control provides a different view of the attack, meaning each control has its way of representing information (i.e., logging). Having more than one logging facility provides better visibility and helps responders understand the context easily. For example, in a network attack, a firewall will log the attack activity differently than an IDS. Looking at the same activity logs on different security controls streamlines the correlation process and makes intrusion analysis easier.

One important thing to note here is that DID was never meant to encourage people to buy more security products thinking that the more security controls, the better the defense! Quality should be the driving force, and having a single security control that's well-configured and hardened is way better than having three consecutive loosely configured controls. Trust us, the last thing you would need to do is bring more solutions to your network, while the existing ones need to be fully and properly utilized.
